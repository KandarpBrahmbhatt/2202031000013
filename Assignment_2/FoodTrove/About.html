    • About The Carrot

    Carrots are root vegetables that belong to the Apiaceae family, which also includes parsley, celery, and parsnips. They are known scientifically as Daucus carota and are native to Europe and southwestern Asia. Carrots are one of the most widely cultivated and consumed vegetables globally, prized for their sweet flavor, crunchy texture, and vibrant orange color.


    Carrots are an excellent source of beta-carotene, a compound that the body converts into vitamin A. They also provide a range of other vitamins and minerals, including vitamin K, vitamin C, potassium, and fiber. Consuming carrots regularly can support eye health, immune function, and overall well-being
